Who We Are?

Our mission is to create opportunities for enrichment, enjoyment, and communication to our clients, through the implementation of telepresence technology and programming. Additionally, we hope to promote innovation for new and profound avenues of human connection.

Watch Our Video Below

WeGo currently connects inpatient pediatric patients with three local Baltimore attractions - the National Aquarium, Maryland Science Center, and Baltimore Center Stage - through telepresence visits. Our program not only strengthens the Baltimore community by creating new connections, but also improves the inpatient experience by positively impacting the children involved. We aim to achieve normalization through socialization, by combating social and psychological obstacles linked to hospitals and illness. With WeGo, pediatric patients gain independence from their medical treatments and transcend the physical boundaries of their inpatient experience. Concurrently, we believe that utilizing technology to create a more united and connected community will strengthen the bonds between institutions in Baltimore, and will break down social barriers for populations such as children at the Johns Hopkins Children's Center and the University of Maryland Children's Hospital

What is Telepresence?

A telepresence robot connects a person to a location through the internet. The robot can explore a location in a way that the operator can see, hear, talk and move around as if they are physically there. Robots like this are used in healthcare, education and business for a variety of purposes. VGo is one of many manufacturers in the telepresence robotics field, and is the model provided to the National Aquarium free of charge during the pilot. VGo is about 4 feet tall and the base is one square foot. It weighs less than 20 pounds. It has navigation lights at the base as well as on the top camera. It doesn’t move faster than walking speed and has an auto-stop sensor when the wheels bump against an object, such as a foot or exhibit.

Our Work

We have teamed with multiple organizations to bring enrichment, enjoyment, and communication to our clients

National Aquarium

Since its founding 1981, the National Aquarium has educated generations of Maryland families. The aquarium is committed to the education and inspiration of Maryland school children, educating over 100,000 local Maryland children a year. The non-profit aquarium’s dedication to Maryland families and children aligns with the WeGo Foundation’s mission for enriching the lives of pediatric inpatients in the Baltimore area. This alignment has furthered the partnership between the two organizations, leading to the development of telepresence tours and programs for children at the Johns Hopkins Children's Center.

Maryland Science Center

Founded in 1976, the Maryland Science Center continues to be an educational and inspirational hub for science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM). Located in the Inner Harbor of Baltimore with an array of interactive demos, hands-on exhibits, and films in it’s IMAX theater, the center never fails to keep families and kids engaged. The center’s commitment to educating the youth of Baltimore and demonstrating the power of the sciences provides a unique opportunity for WeGo Foundation’s clients: to enjoy and to learn, all in one unique telepresence tour at the Maryland Science Center.

Baltimore Center Stage

Launched in 1963 and located in the Mount Vernon Cultural District of Baltimore, Baltimore Center Stage has grown to become Baltimore’s premier and largest professional producing theater. The theatre group offers multiple plays and events, including a family series, however these programs only scratch the surface of the group’s amazing efforts in educating children. Programs such as the Young Playwrights Festival, Drama Club, and the Student Matinee series demonstrate the group’s dedication for inspiring imagination, creativity, and a passion for the arts among Baltimore’s youth.
